  • What is Bitcoin?

    Bitcoin is a consensus-based network that enables a new payment system and all-digital money. It is the first decentralized peer-to-peer payment network powered by its users, with no central authority or middlemen. From the user's perspective, Bitcoin is more or less like internet cash. Bitcoin can also be seen as the largest triple entry bookkeeping system in existence.

  • Who controls the Bitcoin network?

    Nobody owns the Bitcoin network, and no one owns the technology to handle email. Bitcoin is controlled by all network participants in the world. Programmers improve the software, but they cannot force changes to the protocol itself, as all users can choose what software and version they will use. To remain compatible with each other, all users must use software subject to the same rules. Bitcoin can only function properly with full compliance among all users. Therefore, all users and developers are strongly motivated to protect this consensus.

  • Why do people trust Bitcoin?

    Much of the trust in the Bitcoin network is due to the fact that it is not actually required at all. The Bitcoin network is fully open source and decentralized. This means that everyone has access to its source code at all times. Developers around the world can therefore see exactly how Bitcoin works. All transactions and marketed bitcoins can be openly consulted by anyone in real time. All payments can be made without the need for the participation of third parties, and the entire system is protected by cryptographic algorithms that are subject to solid verification by independent experts, tailored to those used in online banks. No organization or individual can control the Bitcoin network, which is secure even if not all of its users are trustworthy.
